Armor Up For The Ride

Your gear is your absolute first line of defense. Click on any piece of gear in the diagram below to learn why it's essential for your survival and performance on the road.

Full-Face Helmet

The most crucial piece of gear you will ever own. A full-face helmet protects not just your skull, but your face and jaw—areas highly prone to impact during a crash. It also shields you from wind, debris, and insects, reducing rider fatigue at high speeds.

  • High-impact EPS liner
  • Aerodynamic shell design
  • Anti-scratch, anti-fog visor
  • ECE/DOT/Snell Certified

Armored Leather Suit

Designed for the track but heavily utilized by aggressive street riders, a full leather suit provides unmatched abrasion resistance. When sliding on asphalt at high speeds, leather is the only material proven to consistently save skin.

  • Premium cowhide/kangaroo leather
  • CE-level internal armor (shoulders/elbows)
  • Stretch panels for mobility
  • Aerodynamic race hump

Back Protector

Your spine is fragile. A dedicated back protector absorbs and distributes the force of an impact across a wider area, preventing catastrophic spinal injuries. It can be worn standalone or slotted into your jacket/suit.

  • CE Level 2 Impact Protection
  • Articulated panels for flexibility
  • Breathable mesh lining
  • Kidney belt integration

Riding Gloves

Human instinct is to put our hands out when we fall. Riding gloves protect against friction burns, broken knuckles, and road debris. Good gloves also enhance your grip on the throttle and brake levers, especially in wet conditions.

  • Carbon fiber knuckle armor
  • Palm sliders (prevents wrist fractures)
  • Reinforced stitching
  • Pre-curved fingers to reduce fatigue

Riding Pants

Standard denim will shred in milliseconds against asphalt. Proper riding pants are reinforced with materials like Kevlar or Aramid fibers and feature built-in armor at the knees and hips, providing crucial slide and impact protection.

  • Abrasion-resistant textiles/leather
  • Adjustable knee and hip armor
  • Weatherproof/waterproof linings
  • Heat shields for exhaust protection

Riding Boots

Your feet and ankles carry the weight of the bike and are highly exposed. Riding boots have reinforced shanks, toe boxes, and heel cups to prevent the foot from being crushed or twisted underneath a downed motorcycle.

  • Anti-crush lateral stiffness
  • Reinforced ankle protection
  • Oil-resistant, high-grip soles
  • Replaceable toe sliders
