Premium Parts & Upgrades

Elevate your ride. Discover the best aftermarket and OEM components to enhance performance, aesthetics, and the long-term reliability of your motorcycle.

The Sum of Its Parts

A motorcycle is only as good as the components that build it. Whether you're replacing worn-out consumables to keep your daily commuter running smoothly, or investing in high-end upgrades for track-day dominance, knowing your parts is essential.

Essential Components

Engine & Drivetrain

From high-flow air filters and spark plugs to heavy-duty chains and sprockets, upgrading your drivetrain ensures efficient power delivery.

Exhaust Systems

Shed weight and gain horsepower. Slip-ons offer a better sound profile, while full exhaust systems drastically improve engine performance.

Brakes & Rotors

Going fast means nothing if you can't stop. Upgrade to sintered brake pads and braided steel lines for a firmer lever feel and reduced fade.

Suspension

The most impactful upgrade you can make. Adjustable forks and rear shocks tailored to your body weight transform how the bike handles corners.

Tires & Wheels

Your only contact with the road. Choose between high-mileage touring compounds, sticky track-day rubber, or rugged off-road knobbies.

Electronics & Lighting

Enhance visibility and safety with LED headlight conversions, integrated tail lights, and advanced battery management systems.

Making the Choice

OEM vs. Aftermarket

Choosing the right parts means understanding the difference between Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) parts built by the factory, and aftermarket alternatives that often offer better performance or lower costs.

How to Source the Right Parts

Follow these steps before making your next purchase to avoid compatibility issues.

1

Identify the Exact Model

Motorcycle parts vary wildly from year to year. Always have your exact make, model, and year (and sometimes VIN) ready before searching for any component.

2

Determine Your Goal

Are you replacing a broken part, doing routine maintenance, or aiming for a performance upgrade? This dictates whether you should buy OEM or look into aftermarket brands.

3

Read the Forums

Don't just trust the manufacturer's claims. Search owner forums for your specific bike to see real-world reviews of the part's longevity and ease of installation.

4

Professional Installation

While bolt-on aesthetics are easy, leave internal engine parts, complex suspension tuning, and critical brake system bleeding to certified professionals if you lack the tools.
